Project description

Forecasting demand before sales history exists.

Fast-fashion products have short lifecycles, sparse sales, and early stock decisions. The project addresses this cold-start problem by learning from multiple signals available at or before launch, while accounting for sales that may be hidden by low stock availability.

01

Purpose

Predict total product-store demand during the first seven selling weeks to support more informed production, allocation, and replenishment decisions.

02

Multimodal features

Fuse product images, BLIP and LLM captions, structured attributes, temporal variables, store context, and internal sales-derived trend sequences.

03

Realistic evaluation

Use a chronological weekly rolling protocol with a 45-day observation gap, 27 unseen test weeks, and a newly trained model for each week.

04

Stock-aware targets

Apply a conservative upward-only correction to early stock-constrained rows, then evaluate the trained model against untouched original sales observations.

Technical approach

From product signals to seven-week demand.

Separate modality encoders are fused into one representation and decoded with historical trend context. Diffusion denoising acts as an auxiliary training objective.

Technologies used

A practical deep-learning research stack.

The implementation combines data preparation, vision-language representation learning, Transformer-based forecasting, gradient boosting baselines, and reproducible experiment analysis.
